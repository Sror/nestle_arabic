Nutrition is all about averages so do not panic if your child does not finish his food every day. The thing about illness and children, particularly younger children, is that they haven’t been around very long and their bodies have not met many of the germs that surround us. Therefore, they get sick often. This leads to an important fact: In order to build immunity, children have to get sick! You can help boost your child’s immunity with the active involvement and cooperation of the person caring for your child – whether that is yourself, a nanny or outside day care – by providing your child with a healthy balanced diet, ensuring good hygiene and, most importantly, insisting on frequent hand washing.
The most important nutrients for immunity are:
  • Protein: Protein is essential for growth, tissue repair and enzyme activity. Animal products (e.g., meat, dairy and eggs) constitute the main source of high-quality protein.
  • Vitamins and minerals: These include Vitamins A, C and E, zinc and selenium. They protect cells in your child’s body from oxidation and defend him against germs and infection. Coloured fruits and vegetables are rich in Vitamins A and C; vegetable oil and avocados are rich in Vitamin E; meat and seafood are rich in zinc and selenium.
  • الحديد: A very important component of red blood cells. It prevents iron-deficiency anaemia and protects your child from infections. Foods that contain high amounts of iron include liver, meat, beans, green vegetables and fortified cereals and some growing-up milks.
  • Fat: A source of concentrated energy, fat also serves as a vehicle for certain vitamins like A, D, E and K. الأحماض الدهنية الأساسية play an important role in building cells and enhancing your child’s immunity.
  • Probiotics and prebiotics: These are ingredients found in fortified foods, such as some growing-up milks for children, and can help build your child’s natural immunity.

Prebiotics are carbohydrates that are not digested or absorbed in the small intestine but remain intact through the digestive tract to reach the large intestine where they essentially serve as food for the beneficial bacteria (microflora) that resides there. Alongside fibre, they play an important role in maintaining your child’s healthy digestive system.
From the time your child begins to crawl, and as he learns to walk and run, curiosity drives much of his activity. He will touch, and even taste, whatever he can get his hands on. This exposes your child to all kinds of bacteria and microbes that can cause stomach upset, irregular bowel movements and diarrhoea that can be severe at times. Prebiotics play a role in treating constipation in children, and research shows that they also can help protect the gut from harmful microorganisms that can lead to intestinal infections.
One good way to help your child stay healthy and keep his immune system strong is to choose his foods wisely and make sure that they include prebiotics.
Where can you find Prebiotics?
Prebiotics are found naturally in fruits and vegetables such as tomatoes, bananas, berries, onions, garlic, artichoke, asparagus, chicory and spinach; it is also present in grains such as wheat and barley; legumes, including lentils, kidney beans, chickpeas, navy beans and soy beans, and Growing-Up milk fortified with prebiotics. So try to include these foods in his diet, and offer your child 2 cups of Growing-up milk that is fortified with Prebiotics.

I hear that toddlers are at risk of having الحديد deficiency Anemia. How to prevent it in my child?

More
الحديد plays a major role in your child’s physical and mental development, as well as strengthening his immune system. Anemia caused by iron deficiency is common among little children. This form of anemia is most often a result of not eating enough foods – such as meat – that contain iron. To prevent your child from having it, offer your child foods rich in iron. These include:
  • Meat, poultry & fish: The most important source of iron and one that is easily absorbed by the body
  • Legumes (e.g., lentils and beans) and green leafy vegetables (e.g., spinach)
    Always add a source of Vitamin C to vegetarian iron-rich sources (Orange, Lemon Juice) to maximize the absorption of الحديد in your child’s body.
  • Fortified foods: Some foods, such as growing-up milk and breakfast cereals, often are enriched with iron. As milk plays a vital role in your child’s daily diet, offer 2 cups of Growing-up milk fortified with iron every day, it helps your child meet his daily requirement of this important mineral.

More
It’s natural to be concerned about protecting baby as she’s exposed to new environments and people. Maintaining a healthy immune system is baby’s best natural defense. During your baby’s first 12 months, she develops immunities and strengthens her resistance. However, the immune system doesn’t fully develop until children are teenagers. That’s why babies need extra care to keep them healthy.
To help keep baby healthy, try these tips:
  1. Breastfeed your baby throughout the first year of life. Breastmilk is the ideal source of nutrition and protection for baby throughout the first year of life, because it contains antibodies that help protect your baby. Breastfeeding also fosters an infection-fighting environment in your baby’s digestive tract that appears to help reduce the incidence of ear infections, allergies, and diarrhoea.
  2. Keep baby’s immunizations up to date. Take your baby for regular checkups, and make sure her immunizations stay on schedule. Talk to your health care professional about the best immunization schedule for your baby.
  3. Allow for plenty of rest. Ask your day care provider about when and where baby will nap. Babies and young children generally get their sleep from a combination of night time sleep and day time naps. Without adequate sleep, the immune system weakens, and baby becomes more vulnerable. Newborn babies need about 16 hours of sleep per day. After 6 months of age and until your child is 3 years old, the sleep requirement decreases slightly to between 10 to 14 hours per day.
  4. Keep baby’s environment clean. Many day care providers successfully limit the spread of colds and flu by frequently washing their hands, as well as the children’s hands, faces, toys, and changing surfaces. Ask about your provider’s policies, and adopt their routines.

The next phase you will notice is that your child will be able to control himself for a longer time. Night-time potty training comes after daytime potty training has been acquired. Make sure not to offer him liquids before going to bed (for at least an hour) and encourage him not to wet his night suit, and when he succeeds, don’t forget to congratulate him.

Toddlers sleep an average of about 12-14 hours a day and take one nap, and it is recommended that toddlers are put on a sleep schedule and have them stick to it every day even during weekends and holidays to ensure a continuation of this pattern. Sleep schedules and routines usually vary according to the child’s age and how much sleep he needs. The following table gives you an example of a sleep schedule for toddlers:

Morning wake-up time Nap time Nap wake-up time Bed time
7:00 AM 11:00 AM 1:00 PM 7:00 PM

Even with the above sleep schedule and a good routine you might still face problems in your child’s sleep, like waking up in the middle of the night crying, asking for milk or visiting your bed if he is old enough to climb out of his. Rest assured, it is normal for children or even adults to wake up in the night. Some toddlers know how to settle themselves back down and some need some assistance. Here are some tips to help your child fall back asleep:
  • Make sure that your baby is not waking up because of a dirty diaper, tight pajamas, feeling hot or cold, or hunger and check if he is ill.
  • If your baby is attached to a sleep aid, like a dummy, a blanket or a doll, make sure that he can find it in his crib when he first wakes up. This way he is able to soothe himself back alone.
  • Avoid including in the sleep routine music or lighting, unless, if he wakes up he will find the same conditions as when he fell asleep. If the music was on, the music needs to be playing when he wakes up!
  • Avoid giving him a bottle of milk if he wakes up; break the rule once and he is already asking for it every time in the middle of the night.
  • Avoid taking him out of his crib, and instead cuddle him or tap his back. When he first cries, wait for a while just in case he falls back to sleep alone. If he fails to do so, comfort him for a short time, and then get out. Return every 5 minutes at first, and then make the gaps bigger until he falls asleep, even if at first, he will cry a bit. This way, he knows that you are there for him, but that he needs to sleep. After a few nights, he will learn to sleep faster.

في أي عمر يستطيع الطفل تناول حليب البقر؟

More
إذا كنت مستمرة بالرضاعة الطبيعية، فتشير التوصيات إلى المثابرة على ذلك بقدر المستطاع. لا يصلح حليب البقر للرضع دون السنة من العمر. أما إذا كان طفلك يحصل على غذاء حليب للرضع أو حليب النمو، فينصح أيضا ً بالمتابعة لأطول فترة ممكنة. ماذا يعني ذلك؟ يعني، انه من الأفضل أن تعطي الأم طفلها ما لا يقل عن 500 مل من حليب النمو حتى يبلغ ما لا يقل عن 3 سنوات، وذلك لأن حليب البقر لا يمد الطفل بكل إحتياجاته الغذائية. فمثلا، ان حليب البقر يحتوي على كمية كبيرة جدا ًمن البروتين، ولا يوفر الأحماض الدهنية الأساسية التي يحتاجها الطفل لنمو دماغه ، كما انه لا يزوده بالفيتامينات وبعض العناصر المهمة الأخرى مثل الحديد والمعادن التي تضاف إلى حليب النمو.

ماذا يجب أن تعطي الطفل الذي يرفض الحليب عند الصباح؟

Growing-up milk plays a major role in your child’s growth and development especially between 1-3 years. Children who continuously avoid milk are exposed to loss in bone density which can cause osteoporosis later in life. 25% of children who avoid milk are prone to bone fractures before 8 years of age, most of them caused by small trips or falls.
Try making milk a little more fun! here are some delicious ways to serve it:
  • Prepare a milkshake by add his favorite fruits such as strawberries
  • Beat the milk with a teaspoon of vanilla flavor
  • Try offering growing-up milk that is already flavored with Honey that babies usually like
  • Add unsweetened cocoa powder (Serve with cold or hot milk) or add strawberry flavor
  • Prepare milk in an attractive cup (with his favorite cartoon picture)
  • Serve him milk with cereals suitable for his age.
Remember, toddlers need 3 milk and dairy products per day. So offer your child 2 cups of his growing- up milk that contains all the essential nutrients for healthy growth, brain development and good immunity, as well as 1 serving of yogurt, labneh or cheese. Include in his diet as well, foods that are naturally rich in Calcium such as Spinach and Broccoli, knowing that the absorption of Calcium is higher from milk.

من المهم جدا أن يتناول الأطفال الخضروات. إعتماداً على فصول السنة، هناك بعض الخضروات التي لا تتوفر إلا بموسمها، والمجمدة من هذه الخضروات هي البديل. ومن المؤكد ان الطازجة هي الأفضل، ولكن لا بأس بالمجمدة، ويجب عدم التردد باستعمالها. تجميد الخضروات لا يجردها من الفيتامينات، المعادن والألياف الأساسية لصحة الطفل!

هل يمكنني إضافة الملح والدهون عل طعام طفلي الذي بلغ السنة من عمره؟

يمكن إضافة الملح بإعتدال. كما السكر تماما، لا يجب الإفراط بإضافته على طعام الأطفال. والأفضل عدم إضافة أي منهما. عودي الطفل على تناول أطعمة قليلة الملح والسكر. ولكن، من المهم إضافة بعض الدهون. الأطفال بحاجة إلى 4-3 ملاعق صغيرة من الدهون الصحية موزعة على وجبات النهار. لذا لا تترددي من إضافة القليل من الزبدة أو ملعقة صغيرة من الزيت النباتي (زيت الذرة، دوار الشمس، الكانولا أو زيت الزيتون) الى وجبتي الغداء والعشاء.
