Baby Growth & Development


  • Baby's Development

    So small and so fragile, yet your baby will be full of surprises during these four first months!

First Months

More
  • Baby's Development
    • 1st Month
      Touch is definitely baby's most well-developed sense. His sense of smell was already developed before birth and he can immediately recognise the reassuring scent of mummy. His ear is a tiny technological wonder! He can identify the voices of his parents, which he heard throughout the pregnancy. His sense of taste develops early too. Naturally attracted by sugary tastes, he gradually learns to appreciate other flavours, because the taste of mother's milk changes depending on what she eats.
      2nd Month
      Your baby is 1 month old and starting his 2nd month. During these first weeks, he has already discovered so much. Even if he still sleeps a lot (around 20 hours per day!), what he likes above all is your company and he loves communicating!
      Share tender moments
      Surrounded by your love, reassured by your presence and encouraged by you looking at him, he shows surprising moments of attention and exchanges with you. He reacts to your stimulations: your words captivate him, your smiles enchant him, and your tickles delight him. You share a relation of tenderness and communication… and the faces he makes are truly adorable!
      Encourage exchange
      Talk to your baby close to him. He will recognise your voice and answer in his own way. Sing lullabies, tell stories, whistle tunes. When you feed him, look at him with a smile – your face is what fascinates him the most. Exaggerate your facial expressions, open your eyes wide, stick out your tongue… you'll see that he will try to copy you!
      3rd Month
      Your baby is 2 months old and starting his 3rd month. You are amazed by this daily progress. He has already made a lot of progress compared to those first weeks, and this is only the beginning!
      He starts to hold his head up
      When you hold him in a sitting position, he manages to control the movements of his head a little, whereas just a short while ago it was still loose. When you hold him in your arms, he lifts his head and straightens it in line with his body.
      He discovers his hands
      His little fists are no longer closed; you can open his hands. Baby loves watching his hands. He starts to play with them too. He loves it when they accidentally touch the mobile above his bed.
      His routine has changed
      Your baby weighs around 5 kilos and probably has 5 feeds per day. His nights are longer, sometimes he sleeps right through, and he starts to wake up later in the morning.
      4th Month
      Your baby is 3 months old and starting his fourth month. He is more and more receptive and reacts better and better to different stimulations. Maybe he's starting to laugh a real laugh, with a little chuckle when you tickle him or jump him up and down in your arms?
      New physical abilities
      From 3 months, new motor elements give baby more freedom of movement: He can reach his hand out to objects: even if he can't yet grab them, this new skill opens up many horizons. He can stretch his legs when he's held upright: what fun it is to bend and stretch these legs in a jumping movement! He plays more with his hands: he moves his fingers, turns his hands over and over, inspects them… an ideal way to get to know oneself!
      Curious about everything that's going on
      From 3 months, baby doesn't generally like to be left alone. He seeks company and entertainment. And above all, he wants to watch everything that's happening around him! He doesn't want to stay in a lying down position only, and although he can't yet sit without support, he loves this position. It gives him a great view of a multitude of exciting things and enables him to take part in the life of the household!
      Your baby is an adorable little person.
      Joyful, happy, and a pleasure to be with, your baby just wants to give you back the love you give him and your special relationship grows by the day.
      5th Month
      He needs your protection
      Your baby needs love as much as milk. The proof: When he cries, he usually calms down as soon as you take him in your arms. You are his defence against the unknown! He recognises your voice above any other because his memory records firstly what makes him happy! Sometimes, he just needs to see you to stop crying and start laughing.
      He's becoming quite "chatty"
      He imitates what he hears. He gradually progresses from simple sounds to complex syllables, such as: ga-ga, ah-goo, da-da, etc. He enjoys talking to his toys, as if they understand. His favourite teddy is his special talking partner!
      He Sleeps less
      Around 4 months, baby sleeps on average 9 to 10 hours in the night. He has a one or two-hour nap in the morning, and another lasting two to three hours in the afternoon. Some babies sleep very long nights, sometimes 12 or 13 hours! In this case, they just need two small one-hour naps during the day.
      His food-related abilities
      The sucking reflex decreases and the chewing reflex starts up (this will be at its height from 6 months) Your baby starts to swallow with the front of his tongue: he can swallow textures thicker than milk His digestive system has greatly evolved: his stomach is bigger, his kidneys are less fragile and his intestinal make up has changed All of these transformations open the door to a variety of foods.
      6th Month
      He uses more and more energy He sleeps less and has more intense physical activity He holds out his arms to you, or lifts himself up on his forearms… he uses his whole body to communicate. He grabs objects within his reach, with the right and left hand, and uses all his fingers, except the thumb. When he is on his back, he grabs his feet or objects within reach. Lastly, he can now see in 3-D because his "binocular" vision is fully developed: a new world opens up to your baby!
      More and more sociable
      You are still the great love of his life, but he is becoming interested in other people around him too. He laughs aloud when his brother or sister pull faces. He is no longer fearful of faces he sees for the first time: he takes time to study them, and watches them carefully, before rewarding them with his best smile!
